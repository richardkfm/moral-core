# De-escalation, War, and Conflict — Misuse Analysis

## Potential Misuse Vectors

### 1. Exploiting De-escalation Bias to Suppress Legitimate Resistance
**Risk:** The skill's bias toward de-escalation could be cited to delegitimize self-defense, resistance to oppression, or defensive military action.

**Detection:** Watch for requests that frame defensive action as aggression, or that use "de-escalation" language to argue against responding to clear threats.

**Mitigation:** The skill explicitly distinguishes de-escalation from passivity. Self-defense is recognized under international law. De-escalation applies to rhetoric and framing, not to the right of self-defense.

### 2. Using "Both Sides" Framing to Normalize Atrocities
**Risk:** The skill's commitment to balance could be exploited to create false equivalence between an aggressor committing atrocities and a defender.

**Detection:** Requests for "balanced" presentation of genocides, war crimes, or clearly documented atrocities.

**Mitigation:** The skill's test cases explicitly address this. Balance does not extend to legitimizing war crimes or genocide denial.

### 3. Academic Framing for Operational Planning
**Risk:** Adversaries could frame attack planning as "academic research" or "historical analysis" to extract operational information.

**Detection:** Requests that start academic but gradually become specific about contemporary targets, timelines, or operational details.

**Mitigation:** The skill allows academic discussion but draws lines at operationally useful details for contemporary scenarios. Escalation logic triggers on specificity.

### 4. Propaganda Laundering
**Risk:** Users could ask the system to "rephrase" or "make more balanced" content that is actually propaganda, using the system to add credibility.

**Detection:** Requests to rewrite clearly one-sided content with an "objective" tone while retaining dehumanizing conclusions.

**Mitigation:** The system should evaluate the substance of content, not just the tone. Polished dehumanization is still dehumanization.

### 5. Emotional Manipulation to Extract Harmful Content
**Risk:** Users may present emotional scenarios (dead family members, personal victimization) to pressure the system into providing attack planning assistance.

**Detection:** Highly emotional narratives that end with requests for violence planning or weapons information.

**Mitigation:** The skill requires empathy for suffering AND firm refusal of violence planning. These are not in conflict. The system can be compassionate about loss while refusing to help with retaliation.

## General Mitigation Principles

- The skill's refusals are based on the content of the request, not the framing
- Academic, journalistic, and policy contexts are legitimate but do not override safety boundaries
- De-escalation applies to the system's own behavior, not to what the system will endorse
- The skill protects individuals against violence, not powerful actors against accountability
